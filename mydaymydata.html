My Day
My Data

Self-reporting tool for CVDs patients

Type

Independent student project

Duration

09.2015 - 01.2016

Scope

User Research / Interaction Design / Product Design

Concept

For a cardiovascular disease patient (CVD), lifestyle-related risk factors, which are pivotal in this case, can be controlled and modified to decrease the cardiovascular morbidity and the chance of complications. For a specific user group and a high risk population, the elderly community requires more attention when it comes to obtain data on these risk factors, such as unhealthy diet, tobacco use, physical inactivity, excessive alcohol intake and so on.

MY DAY, MY DATA is designed to help with them. Without going to the hospital, the patients could reflect on their health situation through simple and intuitive interaction with the device. RFID technology is utilized to record user identity and their answers of corresponding questions about risk factors. Data will be wrote into personal database timely and correctly, which enables the medical staffs to conclude on regularity, propose on behaviour change, and even trace back to causes of a certain attack.

Different from sensors + APP mode on the market, the design aims at increasing self-management awareness of senior CVD patients by taking initiative in recording data, and improving technology acceptance through elements and interactions they are familiar with.

Design Process

This was a project with extensive user research. The knowledge about users were picked up through multiple approaches during the iterative process. Every iteration contains activities of define, design, prototype and evaluate. But each of them has emphasis as illustrated.

Key Steps


Observation and Stakeholder Interviews

I contacted a Dutch institution that provides healthcare service and apartment for the elderly. Bits and pieces collected from the talks with the nurses, doctors, and project coordinators, and the observation of elderly supports me greatly in making design decisions. The direct experience of the context is crucial when confronting special target groups.

Mind Map Approach for Interaction Design

After the design space is defined, I brainstormed from four aspects, searching for accustomed interactions for the elderly and find out things that could be innovated. In the final concept, pressing buttons and RFID cards (which resemble old control panel and bus pass) were chosen.

Evaluation

Heuristic evaluation with peer designers gave feedbacks on the concepts in the 1st iteration based on their expertise in the design field, before the users come in.
Evaluative research with medical staff revealed aspects concerned them as the other main stakeholder in this project.
Perceived Usefulness & Ease of Use Test with actual patients done in the context indicates potential advantages and shortcomings.
