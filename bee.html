Bee @
Home

A public service for bio-diverse city

Type

Independent student project

Duration

04.2016 - 06.2016

Scope

Concept Design / Service Design / Game Design

Brief

This challenge is proposed at Living Data Hackathon 2015 by Open Eindhoven. As the second biggest agricultural products exporters in the world, more than half of the Netherlands' geographical area are utlized as agricultural land, which leads to the result of most of rural areas are coverd by agricultural plants. The loss of biodiversity in Netherlands is substaintially higer than elsewhere in Europe and the world. Therefore it is important to encourage citizens growing diverse plants in their gardens to attract various animals and increase the city biodiversity.

Bee @ Home is a service system that engages citizens in the campaign of building a biodiverse city. The system attracts people using a public video game that awards citizens who accomplished the game task with free seed paper as a trigger. The game, which served as the trigger, is installed in electronic interactive terminals that are placed in crowded public areas like central station, city centers, supermarkets etc. It popularize the theme by bridging growing plants and attracting animals. Free seeds play as the incentives to motivate citizens from Bee @ Home game to its online community, where enables users to update what plants are growing in their back yards, and thus arises the chance of exchanging seeds and therefore contributes to urban biodiversity again. On the other hands, the data collected from the online community provides great value for the study on urban biodiversity distribution for local ecologists and city planners.
