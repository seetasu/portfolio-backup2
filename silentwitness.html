Silent
Witness

Redesign daily objects with materiality notion

Exhibited in DDW 2015

Role in the group

Concept generation, filming & editing, DDW prototype

Duration

11.2014 &
09.2015 - 10.2015

Scope

Concept Design / Product Design

Concept

This project studied in the daily life objects and its materialized characteristics imprinted by interactions we had with them. Affection analysis assisted the study to track down the unique feeling that one material carries. Afterwards, time, which sculptures most objects and enables the personal bonding with objects, was concluded as the abstract notion. The clock named Silent Witness is a result inspired by the notion. For us, a ticking of time is noteless, while it carves and changes us secretly. Concrete, which merely deteriorate with time, witnesses time ticking, while the hours and days passed by leave a hidden trace behind the clock that can only be revealed after a period of time. The clock depicts the contrastive yet unavoidable materiality of time.

Silent witness was invited to the DDW 2015. Based on the concept, I made a new prototype for the exhibition. Working with concrete was an new experience for me, the ratio of water and cement can result in different concrete.
