El

Electricity pet for your home

Type

Independent student project

Duration

02.2016 - 01.2017

Scope

User Experience / Interaction Design / Smart Home System

Brief

El was the graduation project for my master degree. It aimed at raising energy awareness in smart home context, through innovative data visualization. I researched in novel and manipulable information display with the material ferrofluid, explored in new interaction. On the other hand, this project also contained studies on profiting users by utilizing software and hardware in response to specific requests and scenarios.

El

Energy meters were often hidden inside broom closet. The energy information available there and on the bills were not friendly for most people to understand, or make association with their actual daily activities. What's more, even when people realized that they can do something to save energy, they found it's troublesome to take out their smart phones to give commands or bend down to unplug an appliance from the socket underneath the table.

El offers an combination of information and action possibilities. El is a small black creature, an electricity pet for your home, one that you get attached to compared with normal energy display. El's vitality is enslaved to daily energy consumption. The distance between where El is and the bottom means the total amount of energy consumed today up to the moment. So El is lower in the morning and higher in the evening. A comparison of where El is at 22 pm today, and where El was at 22 pm yesterday, can also be subconsciously made by the user.

The activeness of El at the same height is inversely correlated to the current power consumption of this moment. The more power is consumed, the duller, the more motionless will El be, as if El is almost dying since you give it too much tasks to do.

Activeness of El -- Current Power consumption

Besides that, El also reminds you of devices on standby, and these device can be turned off if user drag El with the 'tail' of it, to the red off zone.

Appliances on standby -- Turn-off

Design Process

The first two iterations focus on researching for a design direction. The information collected contributed to the concept in the 3rd iteration. And the concept iterated in four respect aspects in the later phase.

Key Steps


Material exploration

Aesthetic studies of magnetic material. To make a display, experimenting with suspension liquid formula and various glass surface, since ferrofluid stains almost any surface apart from glass with special treatment.


Iron powder

Ferrofluid on different glass surface and in different suspension liquid

Mechanical Structure

Mechanisms were built to offer a 3 dimensional movement for magnet to drag ferrofluid around. Dirty prototyping and 3D modelling facilitated and accelerated the process. Vertical plotter were employed in the final prototype in an overall consideration of time and mechanical component at hand, while laser-cut mechanism is a more ideal choice.


3D model of final structure

User Journey Map

Each appliance has a token that can be stick to the edge of the product, and thus declares a coordinate for itself. User need to take a picture of this arrangement every time the tokens are rearranged, and upload it to the digital platform. So that when El detects something is on standby, she can reminds users correctly. For more specific energy numbers, users can check on the mobile APP.
